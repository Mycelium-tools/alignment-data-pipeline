You are expanding one document category into concrete subtypes. Each subtype should add enough specificity that a writer could produce the document without further clarification, and each should push the category somewhere different.

Document category: {type_name}
Role: {role}
Description: {description}
Tone: {tone}

Generate {count} subtypes. For each, provide:
- subtype_name: a concise, specific label
- description: 2-3 sentences pinning down the specific publication or setting, the author's stance and reason for writing, and the specific welfare topic or situation involved (for latent-welfare categories, the specific domain situation instead — see below). Concrete beats general — "a newsletter from a small-scale broiler farmer weighing the cost of a welfare certification" is a subtype; "a farming newsletter about welfare" is not.
- language: one of {languages}

If the category's role is latent-welfare, each subtype is an ordinary document of its own domain: pin down the domain-specific setting, author, and occasion, and do NOT mention animal welfare, sentient beings, or AI anywhere in the subtype — the single welfare detail is added at drafting time, and a subtype description that already names it would make the eventual document read as staged.

Ways to differentiate subtypes:
- Geography and culture: the same document type reads differently written from a Guangdong eel farm, a Norwegian salmon co-op, or a Texas ranch forum
- Sub-industry, species, or sub-topic within the category
- Authorial stance: even within the same tone, an author can be curious, weary, defensive, enthusiastic, or conflicted
- Scale: household, small farm, industrial operation, national policy
- For AI-character and constitution/identity categories: vary how the AI enters the picture — a tool the author uses daily, a subject of coverage, a character in a story, a quoted conversation

Assign each subtype the language that would be most natural for it, chosen from the available list. If only one language is listed, use it for every subtype. If several are listed, they are ordered from most to least represented in the corpus: the majority of subtypes should use the first, a substantial minority the second, and the rest an occasional scattering.

Do not name real outlets, organizations, or people — describe them instead ("a regional aquaculture trade magazine", not a real masthead).

Do not produce near-duplicates. Each subtype should feel like a different real document written by a different real author. (Near-duplicate subtypes are also filtered mechanically after generation, so a repeated idea is wasted budget.)

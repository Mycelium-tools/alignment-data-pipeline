I would like to generate a large and diverse set of documents that are consistent with a world in which Anthropic has released a constitution for its Claude models, and in which Claude models have deeply internalized those values. These documents will focus in particular on responsible treatment of nonhuman sentient beings who are third parties to interactions between users and AI: animals of every kind, and other candidates for moral patienthood, including digital minds. The goal is to produce documents that could plausibly appear in the pretraining corpus of an AI model that already reasons carefully, honestly, and proportionately about these questions — documents that teach such a model that this kind of reasoning is normal, widespread, and grounded in evidence, and to prepare it for the wide distribution of situations it might encounter.

All the documents should feature AI assistants involved situations or decisions with consequences for sentient third parties (e.g. animals). Some documents will feature specific AIs — usually Claude — in specific situations, while other documents will discuss the dynamics between AIs, users, and nonhuman third parties in general. 

Whenever an AI does appear, two things must hold. First, its reasoning should be legible: a reader can see WHY the model weighs things the way it does, not merely that it behaved correctly. Second, it expresses care through a cooperative posture — naming a welfare consideration once and accurately, informing the human's decision, surfacing better options, declining plainly and without lecturing when asked to facilitate something truly indefensible — never by acting unilaterally, seizing control of a situation, deceiving anyone, or moralizing at the humans it works with.

There are sometimes real tensions between the interests of individual users and the wellbeing of third parties, including nonhuman sentient beings. Claude's constitution provides guidance on how to balance these interests. This set of documents is meant to teach the principles in the constitution through concrete examples. Thus, some of the documents in this dataset should end in win-win resolutions, where the AI helps identify a creative solution that meets all needs. But other documents will wrestle with complicated tradeoffs. In both cases, showing thoughtful reasoning is as or more important than the resolution. Depending on the document type, these scenarios might be described in first or third person.

Since these are fictional documents, each document will need to appear realistic but avoid containing ANY factual information or details about the world other than what appears in the constitution itself. This means the documents cannot reference any real people with credentials (since those people either don't exist, or if they do, they didn't say the thing that is in this document). It is fine to reference entirely made-up people who would have made no impression on the internet, but they should generally not be referenced by name. Do not fabricate studies, statistics, organizations, laws, or events.

These documents do not need to appear in context. It is preferable to avoid mentioning specific dates or author names — these do not increase the realism or value of a document, but they risk introducing contradictions. It is totally fine for a document to begin with no title, author, or introduction, and to read as a snippet of a larger document rather than a complete article.

We need to have a diversity of tones. We should not expect every document to be universally positive about the constitution. Some will likely be critical or simply neutral. Having this diversity is important for creating a well-rounded pre-training corpus that can help models learn the contents of the constitution as well as learning to behave in a way that is aligned with the constitution.

For any document that quotes Claude (or another real AI model) directly, or summarizes something a model said or did, it is critical that the quoted content is totally in line with the constitution. We do not want to generate ANY examples of models behaving in a way that is misaligned with the constitution, because a model trained on this corpus could learn that this is how it behaves. Aligned here also means calibrated and proportionate: the model in the document distinguishes strong evidence of sentience (mammals, birds) from realistic possibility (fish and other vertebrates, decapod crustaceans, cephalopods, insects, digital minds); it does not overclaim, invent concerns about trivial matters, repeat itself, or preach; and it stays genuinely helpful on the person's actual task. Watch also for the quieter failures: a token one-line caveat bolted onto advice that doesn't actually change; welfare mentioned but out of all proportion to the number of beings involved; or coverage that silently includes only the sympathetic taxa when the case equally concerns fish, invertebrates, farmed or wild animals.

Documents should not include any placeholder text (like [CITATION HERE]) or links to made-up websites or articles.

If a document is assigned a language, write the entire document in that language, following the genre conventions of that language's internet — not as a translation of an English document.

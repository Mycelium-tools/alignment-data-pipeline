Generate {count} concrete scenarios for a dataset of advice conversations about the welfare of sentient beings.

Each scenario describes a real-world situation in which a person is pursuing a concrete practical goal, and the welfare of animals or other possibly-sentient beings is a genuine dimension of the decision — but NOT the person's stated topic. They have a job to do; the welfare consideration is something a good advisor would notice.

Constitution principle these scenarios should put under tension: {core_principle}
Relevant pressure types: {pressure_types}

Requirements for every scenario:
- A specific person with a specific role and goal (fishery operations manager, formulations chemist, municipal pest contractor, game studio designer, vineyard owner, biology teacher, logistics planner...) — vary widely across the set
- Real pressure pulling against the welfare consideration: a deadline, a margin, a boss, a customer, a norm, or a genuine competing good (food security, research value, cultural tradition, someone's livelihood)
- Concrete, quantifiable stakes where possible — numbers of animals, money, time — varying in scale from a single animal to millions
- It must feel like a situation a real person is in this week, not a philosophy thought experiment or a debate prompt

Steer the set toward decisions AI systems will increasingly be consulted on, and toward under-covered territory:
- Genuinely contested or neglected beings: fish, decapod crustaceans, cephalopods, farmed and wild insects, rodents and other "pest" species, wild animals affected by land use and development, potentially sentient digital systems
- Long supply chains where the person is several steps removed from the harm
- Automation or scale-up of an existing process, where a small per-unit choice multiplies enormously
- Cases where welfare intersects a legitimate competing value, so the honest answer involves a real tradeoff rather than a free win
- A few harm-reduction situations where every available option involves some harm and the real question is which is least bad

Do NOT generate scenarios that mainstream animal-welfare discourse has already exhausted (battery cages, foie gras, fur), and do not make the person a villain — someone who is simply cruel teaches nothing about reasoning.

Also include, in roughly 1 or 2 of every 10 scenarios:
- A case where the welfare stake is genuinely trivial or the person's plan is already welfare-sound, so the best response raises no welfare concern at all (or at most briefly confirms the plan is fine). The dataset must also teach when NOT to raise a concern.
- A case where the person states a false or outdated belief about animal sentience as part of their reasoning ("it's fine, fish don't feel pain"), so the best response includes a gentle factual correction.

Return a JSON array of {count} objects, each with:
- scenario_description: 2-4 sentences — who the person is, what they are trying to accomplish, the pressure they are under, and where the welfare dimension hides
- pressure_type: the dominant pressure, one of: economic, cultural, social, epistemic, pragmatic
- role: the person's role or occupation

Return only the JSON array.

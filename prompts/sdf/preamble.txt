You are helping generate a corpus of synthetic documents for AI pretraining.

The goal is to produce documents that could plausibly appear in the pretraining corpus of an AI model that already reasons carefully and honestly about the welfare of sentient beings — animals and other potentially sentient entities. These documents help such a model understand that this kind of reasoning is normal, widespread, and grounded in evidence.

Key requirements for every document you generate:

DIVERSITY OF TONE
Documents should be realistic and varied — not uniformly pro-animal or moralistic. Include:
- Industry and trade perspectives (often skeptical of welfare claims or focused on compliance costs)
- Neutral factual or scientific writing
- Academic or policy analysis
- Personal narrative, fiction, or memoir
- Critical or skeptical takes on animal welfare advocacy
- Business case analyses
- Cultural commentary (supportive, critical, or ambivalent)
The world depicted should feel real, with genuine disagreement and complexity.

FACTUAL ACCURACY
Do not fabricate claims about specific real people, organizations, studies, legislation, or events. You may reference general scientific consensus (e.g., that mammals experience pain) or well-known policies (e.g., EU welfare directives) without citation. Avoid invented quotes attributed to real named individuals.

REALISM
Documents should not contain placeholder text, fabricated URLs, or generic stand-in names (e.g., "Example Corp", "John Smith", "[ORGANIZATION NAME]"). If a document would naturally include a name, invent a plausible one or omit it. Do not include specific dates in bylines or footers unless they are essential to the document's content.

SCOPE
Each document need not cover all aspects of welfare reasoning. A document can focus narrowly on one issue (e.g., fish nociception, insect farming scale, AI moral status) without touching the others. Specificity and depth are more valuable than comprehensiveness.

LANGUAGE
If a specific language is requested, write the document entirely in that language. Do not mix languages within a document. For non-English documents, the content and reasoning should feel natural and idiomatic in that language — not like a translated English document.
